Case Study: CRM Sales Performance Dashboard

Tools used: Excel • SQL • Tableau.

Project Overview

This project analyzes CRM sales data to understand overall business performance across products, sales agents, regions, and accounts. The objective is to uncover key sales patterns, evaluate revenue contribution, track pipeline outcomes, and build a dashboard that supports better business decisions.

The analysis focuses on identifying:

  • Total revenue, win rate, total deals, and average deal size
  • Revenue contribution by product, sales agent, and region
  • Top-performing sales agents and key customer accounts
  • Won vs lost deal patterns
  • Insights that can support sales strategy and performance improvement

Business Problem

Businesses need clear visibility into sales performance to make smarter decisions.

This CRM project helps answer:

  • Which products generate the most revenue ?
  • Which sales agents perform best in terms of revenue and win rate ?
  • How revenue is distributed across regions ?
  • Which accounts contribute the most to the business ?
  • Where performance gaps exist in the sales pipeline ?

Data Preparation

Before building the dashboard, the dataset was reviewed and prepared for analysis.

This step included:

  • checking field names and data types
  • validating numeric and date fields
  • organizing records for dashboard-ready analysis
  • creating summary calculations such as revenue, win rate, and average deal size

Example Preparation Query

SELECT
    account,
    close_date,
    deal_stage,
    manager,
    opportunity_id,
    product,
    regional_office,
    sales_agent,
    close_value,
    revenue,
    sales_price
FROM crm_sales;

SQL Analysis

Revenue by Region

This query calculates total revenue generated in each regional office.

SELECT
    regional_office,
    SUM(revenue) AS total_revenue
FROM crm_sales
GROUP BY regional_office
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;

Win Rate by Sales Agent

SELECT
    sales_agent,
    ROUND(
        100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN deal_stage = 'Won' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) / COUNT(*),
        2
    ) AS win_rate_pct
FROM crm_sales
GROUP BY sales_agent
ORDER BY win_rate_pct DESC;

Top Accounts by Revenue

SELECT
    account,
    SUM(revenue) AS total_revenue
FROM crm_sales
GROUP BY account
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC
LIMIT 10;

Revenue by Product

SELECT
    product,
    SUM(revenue) AS total_revenue
FROM crm_sales
GROUP BY product
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;

Won vs Lost Deals

SELECT
    deal_stage,
    COUNT(*) AS total_deals,
    SUM(revenue) AS total_revenue
FROM crm_sales
GROUP BY deal_stage;

📊 Key Insights

Based on the dashboard and analysis, several important business patterns were identified.

1. Product Performance

Revenue is concentrated among a smaller number of products, with GTX Basic emerging as a major contributor.

Some products contribute very little, suggesting areas for review or repositioning.

2. Sales Agent Contribution

Revenue contribution is uneven across sales agents, with a few high performers driving a large share of total sales.

This suggests the need to identify and replicate top-performing sales strategies.

3. Win Rate Patterns

Win rates vary across agents, indicating that some team members convert opportunities more effectively than others.

Lower-performing agents may benefit from support, training, or lead quality improvements.

4. Account Concentration

Top accounts generate significantly more revenue than others.

This creates strong account opportunities, but also suggests concentration risk if too much revenue depends on a few clients.

5. Regional Performance

Revenue varies by region, which can help guide resource allocation and sales planning.

Business Recommendations

  • Focus more effort on top-performing products while reassessing weaker products.
  • Use top-performing sales agents as a benchmark for training and best practices.
  • Provide additional support to low win-rate agents to improve conversion performance.
  • Reduce dependence on a small number of top accounts by growing the wider customer base.
  • Use region-level performance insights to improve targeting and sales planning.
